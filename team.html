Game Theory

When I was younger, it felt like games had no rules. Chaos was everywhere and I wasn't told what I had to do to enjoy a game, I was able to play the style I had most fun with.

This was especially true in games like Ultima Online and Diablo 2 hardcore where I was an avid PK and received much pleasure in the demise and suffering of other less skilled players. (Schadenfreude)

Now it feels like most games are care bear style, designed solely for the casual player. I'm not saying anything against casual players, I just think hardcore players who want risk and adrenaline in their games have been left in the cold by gaming companies in search for greater profits.

I hope to help remedy that.

Game Theory

I believe a thriving community is what really makes a game great and gives it endless replay value. I want to focus in on community interaction and hearing what sort of feedback the EoE community has in regards to it.

I've played several games now where it feels like the gaming companies pretend they have the community's best interest in mind but refuse to change anything until there is a massive outcry or make considerable changes to the game without consulting the gamers who play it first.

Another thing that is of high importance to me is user interaction. A player should always have something to do or discover in the game and should never feel like they're just waiting for the next thing to happen. That's boring.
